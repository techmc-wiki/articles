# #13 Flying Machine Basics

---

After two full lessons, we're finally going to make a machine that can move!

In this lesson, we'll only learn about and build some simple flying machines. We won't dive too deep into analyzing flying machine motion, so it will be much easier to learn than the previous two lessons.

## Principle

_Step on your right foot with your left foot and spiral up to the sky!_

High school physics and momentum completely destroyed our martial arts dreams. Fortunately, Minecraft blocks don't have the concept of momentum. So we can perform the following operation.

![img.png](img/1.3.1-原理1.png)

As shown in the figure, we've achieved the "stepping on the right foot with the left foot" process. During this process, the position of the regular Piston acting as the "right foot" doesn't change.

![img.png](img/1.3.1-原理2.png)

As shown in the figure, this is the process of pulling back the "right foot". During this process, the sticky piston's position doesn't change.

Through these two processes, the entire structure moves forward by one block. Let's connect them together.

![img.gif](img/1.3.1-原理3.gif)

By inserting a circuit that activates in sequence into the flying machine body, we get a simple flying machine.

## Simple Flying Machines

### a) 9gt Unidirectional Flying Machine

As shown in the figure, this is a very easy-to-understand 9gt unidirectional flying machine.

![img.png](img/1.3.2-9gt.png)

The right-facing one is a regular Piston, the left-facing one is a sticky piston. Taking right as forward, break the topmost Block of Redstone to start it.

Let's use `/tick freeze` and `/tick step` to observe how this flying machine operates.

![img.png](img/1.3.2-9gt运行.png)

As shown in the figure, this is the operating state of the flying machine. The sticky piston first pulls back the rear section; then the rear regular Piston, under the action of the Block of Redstone, pushes out the front section; after the front section is in place, the Observer responds, and the sticky piston pulls back the rear section again, completing the cycle.

_This flying machine has a period of 9gt_

### b) 10gt Observer Bidirectional Flying Machine

In the above flying machine, the regular Piston is activated by a Block of Redstone, while the sticky piston is activated by an Observer. Let's change them both to Observer activation, as shown in the figure.

![img.png](img/1.3.2-10gt单向.png)

The operation process is no different from the above flying machine, except the period changed from 9gt to 10gt.

Since the Observer signal length is relatively short, it can make the sticky piston only push out without pulling back, so we can replace the rear regular Piston with a sticky piston without affecting its motion.

![img.png](img/1.3.2-10gt双向.png)

This flying machine is centrally symmetric, so if it can move right, moving left is also feasible. This is the simplest bidirectional flying machine.

Almost all simple flying machines in Slime Tech are 10gt flying machines, variants of this flying machine.

![img.png](img/1.3.2-TNT.png)

_The figure shows the bomber section of Mango's World Eater. You can see that this bomber just changed the positions of the pistons, Observers, and Slime Blocks - the abstract structure is the same_

### c) 12gt Piston BUD Flying Machine

![img.png](img/1.3.2-12gt.png)

_In the figure, the three pistons from left to right are: regular Piston, sticky piston, regular Piston_

Before version 1.11 when there were no Observers, this type of flying machine was popular, but it's basically no longer used now. Those with spare capacity should research on their own how to start it and its operation process.

## Mounting and Extension

Since the Slime Tech community has never had unified standards, only some conventional terms, I'll categorize them using my own naming conventions.

### a) Mounting

For the above 10gt bidirectional flying machine, we can change the structure's shape and then attach some other blocks as mounting. For example, the above "bomber section of Mango's World Eater" was obtained by attaching blocks to a 10gt flying machine as mounting.

Similarly, mounting can also attach additional Observers and pistons, giving your flying machine the ability to interact with blocks. We can also implement some interesting functions.

![img.png](img/1.3.3-坟头机.png)

_The figure shows a flying machine called "Grave Marker" that can convert 6-high pillars into a specific shape_

The principle is to mount several pistons in specific directions on a 10gt simple flying machine. This basically doesn't involve knowledge about flying machines, please try it yourself.

![img.png](img/1.3.3-坟头机原理.png)

**Homework: Please design a machine yourself that converts 4-high pillars into the following statue**

![img.png](img/1.3.3-hw1.png)

### b) Extension

Since we can mount Observers and pistons to implement additional functions, but due to the 12-block push limit of a single piston, there are still limitations in implementing some functions. Therefore, we can extend the structure through pistons.

Based on a simple flying machine, we can push forward structures through forward-facing regular Pistons, or pull back rear structures through backward-facing sticky pistons, as shown in the figure:

![img.png](img/1.3.3-拓展.png)

_In the figure, from left to right, from top to bottom, they are: sticky piston, sticky piston, regular Piston, sticky piston, regular Piston_

This flying machine, based on a simple flying machine, extends forward with a 12-block dual TNT duplicator, and extends backward beyond the screen.

![img.gif](img/1.3.3-拓展运行.gif)

From now on, simple flying machines will be directly called "engines", and the concept of "mounting" also includes "extension". Understanding these, you've already stepped through the door of flying machines. The knowledge that follows, broadly speaking, is various kinds of engines, various kinds of mounting and extension, and how to elegantly integrate multiple flying machines together. Keep it up!
